# Jurassic Park / Jurassic World Universe — Dinosaur Cloning Reference

> **Scope:** All 37 species featured across *Jurassic Park*, *The Lost World*, *JP3*, the *Jurassic World* trilogy, and *Jurassic World: Camp Cretaceous*. Dates and notes are drawn from in-universe canon sources (films, *Masrani Global* website, Jurassic World: Rebirth lore).

---

### 17. Tyrannosaurus rex (Rexy and subsequent specimens)
- **Cloning Date:** 1988 (Rexy's batch; first confirmed T. rex)

- **Appearances:** *Jurassic Park*, *The Lost World*, *Jurassic World*, *Jurassic World: Fallen Kingdom*, *Jurassic World: Dominion*

### 18. Velociraptor
- **Cloning Date:** 1991 (successful batch)
- **Cloning Facility:** Isla Sorna (InGen)
- **Notes:** Earlier attempts in ~1986–1990 failed — specimens died after approximately six days from karyolysis. Per the Masrani Global website, this was caused by genetic incompatibility between the raptor genome and the yellow-banded Poison Dart Frog used as gap-fill DNA. Dr. Henry Wu switched to the common Reed Frog, producing a stable result in 1991. This Reed Frog DNA was subsequently used for other species, and is responsible for the sex-change capability seen in later animals.
- **Appearances:** *Jurassic Park*, *The Lost World*, *Jurassic Park III*, *Jurassic World*, *Jurassic World: Fallen Kingdom*, *Jurassic World: Dominion*, *Jurassic World: Camp Cretaceous*

---

### 19. Corythosaurus
- **Cloning Date:** Preliminary genome (97% mapped) by 1997; fully cloned 1999
- **Cloning Facility:** Isla Sorna (InGen, then illegal operation)
- **Notes:** A corythosaurus appears briefly on Roland Tembo's information sheet in *The Lost World* (1997), suggesting its genome was at least partially mapped and possibly an early specimen existed. It was definitively and fully cloned in 1999 as part of the illegal operation on Sorna. Originally had 97% of its genome mapped at time of *The Lost World*.
- **Appearances:** *Jurassic Park III* (confirmed), *The Lost World* (information sheet)

## Notes on Hybrids and Succession

- **First hybrid:** Scorpios Rex (2009), per Dr. Wu's own account.
- **Rebirth hybrids (Mutadon, D-Rex):** Likely created between late 2009 and 2010, after Scorpios Rex, and before the Camp Cretaceous D-Rex incident caused the island's abandonment. However, Wu may not have been aware of these, or may not classify them as "successful" hybrids, meaning they could theoretically predate 2009.
- **Sex-change capability:** Stems from Reed Frog DNA introduced in 1991 for the Velociraptor line; later used across many species.
- **Post-2015 cloning:** After Jurassic World's destruction, no new species could be created on Nublar. Wu continued his work off-island (Indoraptor) and later at Biosyn.
